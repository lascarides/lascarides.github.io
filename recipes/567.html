Recipes » Main Course Butternut Dhal

Ingredients

300 g onion diced
200 g carrots diced
100 ml olive oil
400 g butternut diced 1cm
1 bay leaf
200 g celery diced
4 cloves garlic crushed
10 g ginger microplane grated
50 g tomato paste
400 g lentils dried split red, soaked for 1hr in cold water
800 g tomatoes chopped (2 cans)
800 ml water
2 cups jasmine rice to serve
1 Tbsp cumin seed
1 tsp coriander seed
1 tsp fennel seed
1 Tbsp black peppercorns
2 Tbsp turmeric

Instructions

  1. Toast the whole spices in a dry cast iron pan on a medium to high heat until fragrant. Cool, then grind to a fine powder in a mortar and pestle. Fold through the powdered turmeric.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, bay leaf and EVO to a large heavy based pot and begin to sweat on a medium heat until onions are translucent.
  3. Add the diced carrots, celery, ginger and butternut. Increase the heat and continue to stir.
  4. Add spice mix, tomato paste and coat the vegetables in this paste before adding the soaked and rinsed lentils, chopped tomatoes and filtered water.
  5. Bring back to a simmer and continue to cook for a further 35 minutes until lentils are tender and the tomato has broken down to form a gravy like consistency.
  6. Rinse your rice through a strainer or colander then add rice to a large pot of boiling water and boil uncovered for 30 minutes. Drain, then add back into the pot with the lid on (off heat) for 5 minutes to ‘steam’. Fluff up with a fork when you’re ready to serve.
  7. Season dhal with salt and serve with plenty of virgin olive oil, fresh herbs from the garden and your fragrant jasmine rice.
