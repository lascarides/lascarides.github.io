Recipes » Main Course Okonomiyaki

Ingredients

120 g flour
to_tastes sea salt
1/4 tsp sugar
1/4 tsp baking powder
75 g tofu silken, or nagaimo
180 ml dashi
500 g cabbage shredded
150 g bacon
4 eggs
1/2 cup tenkasu or agedama, optional
1/4 cup pickled ginger
1 Tbsp canola oil
30 g bulldog sauce
10 g bonito flakes
10 g wakame dried
30 g japanese mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Prepare Okonomiyaki Batter: In a large bowl, combine 1 cup (120 g) all-purpose flour, ¼ tsp. salt, ¼ tsp. sugar, and ¼ tsp. baking powder and mix all together.
  2. If using nagaimo: Peel and grate nagaimo in a small bowl. It can get itchy, so work quickly and rinse your hand right after. Nagaimo is very slimy and slippery, so make sure you have a good grip if you wear a glove.
  3. Add the tofu or nagaimo and dashi in the bowl.
  4. Mix all together till combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour.
  5. Remove the core of the cabbage and mince it. Set aside to let the moisture evaporate so it won’t dilute the batter.
  6. Take out the batter from the refrigerator and add 4 large eggs, ½ cup (8 Tbsp.) tempura scraps (Tenkasu/Agedama), and ¼ cup (4 Tbsp.) pickled red ginger (Kizami Beni Shoga) in the bowl. Mix well until well-combined.
  7. Add chopped cabbage to the batter 1/3 at a time. Mix well before adding the rest.
  8. In a large pan, heat vegetable oil on medium heat. When the frying pan is hot (400F/200C), spread the batter in a circle on the pan. We like thicker okonomiyaki (final thickness is ¾ inches (2 cm)). If you’re new to making okonomiyaki, make a smaller and thinner size so it’s easier to flip.
  9. Place 2-3 slices bacon on top of Okonomiyaki and cook covered for 5 minutes.
  10. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip over.
  11. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix the shape and keep it together. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  12. Flip over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes. If you’re going to cook next batch, transfer to a plate.
  13. Apply bulldog sauce with brush, add Japanese mayonnaise in zigzagging lines (optional), and sprinkle dried bonito flakes (katsuobushi). You can also put dried green seaweed (aonori), chopped green onions, and pickled red ginger on top for garnish.
  14. If you have a Japanese griddle with a lid (We call it “Hot Plate”), you can cook several okonomiyaki at once! Otherwise, I recommend cooking two okonomiyaki (each in one frying pan) at a time.
  15. Okonomiyaki freezes well. Once it cools down (no sauce or toppings), wrap each okonomiyaki in aluminum foil and put it in a freezer bag. When you want to eat it, defrost first and put it in a toaster oven or oven to heat it up. It's a great quick meal!
