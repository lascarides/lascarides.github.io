Recipes » Lunch Spicy Chicken Katsu Sandwiches

Ingredients

1/2 cup mayonnaise
1/4 cup pickles chopped bread-and-butter pickles
1/4 cup hot sauce such as crystal
1 tsp kosher salt plus more
1/2 cup flour
3 Tbsp cayenne pepper
2 tsp garlic powder
1 egg large
2 cups panko
4 chicken thighs skinless, boneless, lightly pounded to ½" thick
1/4 cup olive oil or more
4 rolls hawaiian
1 cup lettuce shredded iceberg

Instructions

  1. Combine mayonnaise, pickles, and 2 Tbsp. hot sauce in a small bowl. Season with salt; set aside.
  2. Combine flour, cayenne, garlic powder, and remaining 1 tsp. salt in a medium bowl. Whisk egg and remaining ¼ cup hot sauce in another medium bowl. Place panko on a large plate. Working one at a time, dip chicken into flour dredge, turning to coat. Shake off any excess. Dip into egg mixture until coated, shaking off excess. Dredge in panko, turning to coat. Transfer to a large plate or a small rimmed baking sheet.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high until shimmering. Working in 2 batches, cook chicken, reducing heat to medium if it’s browning too quickly, until golden and very crisp on both sides, about 3 minutes per side. Transfer to a wire rack.
  4. Spread reserved spicy mayo on one side of each slice of bread. Top with chicken and shredded lettuce, then close up sandwiches.

Prep time • Cook time

These sandwiches combine the shattering panko crispiness of katsu-style cutlets and the fiery heat found in Nashville-style hot chicken with some cooling shredded lettuce to put out the fire.
