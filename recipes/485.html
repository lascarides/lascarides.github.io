Recipes » Main Course Recipe: Slow Cooker Black Bean Enchiladas

Ingredients

1 yellow onion small (or 1/2 large onion) diced
1 capsicum sweet, small, diced
1 can black beans drained and rinsed
1 cup corn frozen
2 tsp chilli powder
1 tsp cumin
1 tsp kosher salt
1.5 cups cheese monterey jack, colby, or other melting cheese, shredded and divided
2 jars salsa any kind
15 tortillas
1 cup chicken or any leftover meat, or extra veggies: pork, hamburger, beef, baked tofu, or roasted vegetables

Instructions

  1. Serves 6 to 8
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together the onion, pepper, black beans, corn, spices, meat (if using), and just 1/2 cup of the cheese. Spread a cup or so of salsa over the bottom of a 6-quart (or larger) slow cooker, enough to evenly coat the bottom with a bit of sauce. (See the Recipe Note below for cooking in smaller slow cookers.)
  3. Fill each of the tortillas with about 1/3 cup of the filling, roll them up as tightly as you can, and pack them seam-side down into the bottom of the slow cooker. Don't worry if some filling falls out — just tuck it back in once you've set the tortilla down.
  4. When you complete the layer, spread another cup of salsa over the top and sprinkle with about 1/2 cup of the remaining cheese. Continue rolling the remaining tortillas with the remaining filling to make one more layer; if you have leftover filling, sprinkle this over the top. Finish by pouring another cup of salsa over the top of the enchiladas. Reserve the last 1/2 cup of cheese to add later.
  5. Place the lid on the slow cooker and cook on HIGH for 2 to 4 hours. In the last 15 minutes of cooking, sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of cheese over the top, re-cover the slow cooker, and finish cooking.
  6. Serve the enchiladas with any remaining salsa. Leftovers will keep for up to a week in the refrigerator.
  7. This recipe was originally published September 2010.
